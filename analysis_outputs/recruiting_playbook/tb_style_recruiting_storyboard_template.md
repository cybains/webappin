# TB-Style Recruiting Storyboard Template

## Purpose
Create conversion-focused recruiting visuals that explain *why this role now* using a clear causal flow.

Core pattern:
`Attention -> Action -> Reaction -> Outcome -> Insight -> CTA`

## Visual Language Rules
- Use one primary message per slide.
- Spotlight only 1-2 focal elements (attention anchoring).
- Use arrows to show cause-effect.
- Reveal information in sequence, not all at once.
- Keep consistent color mapping:
  - Demand pressure: orange/red
  - Candidate supply / unemployment: blue/purple
  - Opportunity / offer: green
  - Neutral context: slate/gray
- Max text density:
  - Headline: 6-10 words
  - Subline: 10-18 words
  - Bullet list: 3-5 bullets

## Data Inputs
- Market demand:
  - `04_annual_vacancy_construction.csv`
- Supply pressure:
  - `02_annual_unemployment_youth.csv`
- Role sheet:
  - title, location, openings, requirements, pay, support, CTA

## Slide Structure (5-Slide Carousel)

### Slide 1 — Market Hook
- Goal: stop scroll with one macro tension.
- Visual: country trend vs EU average (line or slope).
- Copy:
  - Headline: demand pressure statement.
  - Subline: latest comparable years and source.
- Output signal:
  - “Demand is real and persistent.”

### Slide 2 — Mismatch Proof
- Goal: show demand + available labor can coexist.
- Visual: mismatch scatter (x: youth unemployment, y: vacancy).
- Attention anchor: target country label.
- Output signal:
  - “This is not just low demand; it is matching friction.”

### Slide 3 — Role Fit
- Goal: make candidates self-qualify quickly.
- Visual: checklist card with role-specific filters.
- Copy:
  - “You fit if…” bullets.
  - “Not this role if…” bullet.
- Output signal:
  - Better lead quality.

### Slide 4 — Offer Clarity
- Goal: reduce trust friction.
- Visual: compensation + support card.
- Include:
  - salary, bonuses, contract type, relocation/visa process.
- Output signal:
  - “Offer is concrete and transparent.”

### Slide 5 — Process + CTA
- Goal: immediate action.
- Visual: 4-step timeline.
- Copy:
  - “Message keyword + country”
  - “We handle permits/admin”
- Output signal:
  - lead conversion.

## 15-Second Video Version
- 0-2s: Hook (market tension)
- 2-8s: Evidence (trend + mismatch)
- 8-12s: Role + offer
- 12-15s: CTA and keyword

## Quality Checklist
- Does each slide make one claim only?
- Can a viewer understand within 2 seconds?
- Are all numbers sourced or derivable?
- Is the CTA explicit?
- Is visual hierarchy obvious on mobile?
